> about.

I am an undergraduate student at North Carolina State University, majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics. I currently work as an undergraduate research assistant in the Hier Lab, where I research Sensor Fusion and SLAM techniques for legged robots in unpredictable enviroments.

My acedimic interest include Animaloid Robots, Embodied AI, and Safe Autonomey. I aim to develop robots to safely assist humans in challenging, dangerous, or otherwise inaccessible tasks. Besides robotics, I am also interested in Software Systems and Programming Languages.

In my free time, I enjoy playing video games, reading, improving my cooking, playing the bass, and exercising.

I am also a recipient of the Goodnight Scholarship, where I serve as a peer mentor and tutor for new transfer students.

> research.

My research focuses on developing autonomous robots capable of operating safely and effectively in complex, human-centered environments. I'm particularly interested in enabling robots to assist with daily tasks currently inaccessible to many people. Applications of these Robots include helping older adults with household chores, expanding mobility options for those with physical limitations, or handling routine errands for busy families. The common thread among these types of Robots is that they must perceive and reason about dynamic environments while safely navigating around humans.

My current work explores reliable perception and localization techniques for Robots with disrupted or obscured sensors. This research aims to create intelligent systems that can operate reliably in the unpredictable scenarios of everyday life.
